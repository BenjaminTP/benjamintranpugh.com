## Overview

While researching how different internal combustion engines work, I found engineering drawings of V6 components — so I modeled the whole engine. I had just gotten access to Autodesk Inventor, and this became my crash course in it.

<Figure src="v6-engine/hero.jpg" caption="V6 ASSEMBLY RENDER" />

## Process

Working from the component drawings, I modeled each part and built up the full assembly, then used Inventor Studio for rendering and animation of the moving engine.

<Figure src="v6-engine/internals.jpg" align="right" caption="CRANKSHAFT AND PISTON DETAIL" />

## Result

A complete, animated V6 model — and a much deeper understanding of what every component in an engine actually does.

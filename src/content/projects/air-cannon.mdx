## Overview

The assignment was a tennis-ball launcher with a maximum range of 10 meters. We decided that wasn't enough and built a pneumatic cannon that launches projectiles over 60 meters.

<Figure src="air-cannon/live.png" caption="THE FINISHED CANNON — PRESSURE CHAMBER, VALVE, AND BARREL" />

## Build

<Figure src="air-cannon/cad.png" align="right" caption="CAD MODEL" />

The cannon is built from PVC piping, wood, duct tape, and epoxy, pressurized with a bicycle tire pump. Getting there meant pressure calculations to size the chamber, CAD models in Fusion 360 and Onshape, and a lot of hands-on work — hand sawing, PVC solvent welding, and epoxy joints.

## Result

Sixty-plus meters of range from a hand-pumped chamber, and a hard lesson in the difference between a CAD model and a thing you can actually manufacture.
